## Process Lessons (already in audit report, restated here for handover context)

1. A code-review that fixes a metric in one place must grep for every call site of that metric. The morning's audit fixed `memorize_with_merge` but never searched for other `_token_overlap` callers.
2. Data-safety invariants (scope boundaries) must be function-level, not call-site-level. The `scope="all"` CLI flag was a back door.
3. "First real-corpus dry-run" is a mandatory gate for any compounding feature — template dominance only shows up on real content, not hand-crafted test cases.
4. Per-candidate iteration beats top-1 ranking when the ranking metric is one of N gate conditions.
